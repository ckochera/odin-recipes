Overnight White Bread

Description

This is a classic no-knead bread recipe that is sure to impress your taste buds. Even more impressive is the small amount of work that results in huge taste.

The receipt below is sourced from Ken Forkish's book Flour, Water, Salt, Yeast.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams white bread flour
  • 380 grams water, approximately 90-97 degrees Farenheit
  • 11 grams salt (I like to use Kosher salt)
  • 0.4 grams active dry yeast

Steps

  1. Start with the bread flour in a large bowl. Pour the water on top and use your hands to combine
  2. Start a timer for 20-25 minutes and let the flour hydrate
  3. Once the flour has been hydrated, pour the salt and yeast on top of the mixture
  4. Combine using the Pincer method
  5. The bread will need 3-4 folds in the time directly after mixing. If you are unfamiliar with folding, here is a good video
  6. Cover the bowl and let rise 12 hours

After the bread has risen...

  1. Place a dutch oven inside your oven and preheat to 475 degrees Farenheit
  2. Gently dump the bread dough onto a floured surface and shape into a ball
  3. Place the ball into a proofing basket and let proof for approximately an hour
  4. Once proofed, place the dough ball into the dutch oven. Take care as the dutch oven will be very hot
  5. Bake for 30 minutes covered, then remove the dutch oven lid and bake for 10-20 minutes, until the surface of the bread is golden brown
  6. Remove from dutch oven and let cool on a wire rack for at least 45 minutes
